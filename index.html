A Note on a Key Issue: The "Guilty Mind" vs. Accidental Conduct

As you review the reform ideas, please keep one important concept in mind:

  • The Problem: In Arizona, you can be convicted of a "strict liability" crime for an honest mistake. This is because if a law doesn't mention intent (mens rea, or a "guilty mind"), our legal system assumes none is required to be proven.
  • A Proposed Solution: One reform idea you'll see in the "Prosecution & Courts" section aims to fix this. It would change the default so that prosecutors must prove a person acted "knowingly." This ensures our laws target intentional conduct, not accidents, restoring a fundamental principle of fairness.
